**Problem Summary:**

The problem requires finding the largest positive integer that divides two given positive integers without leaving a remainder. The input consists of two strictly positive integers, and the output should be their GCD. The problem statement also notes that while efficiency isn't a primary concern, the solution shouldn't be excessively slow.

**Solution Approach:**

The provided solution implements the Euclidean algorithm for finding the GCD. This algorithm is based on the principle that the greatest common divisor of two numbers does not change if the larger number is replaced by its difference with the smaller number. This process is repeated until the two numbers are equal, which is then the GCD. The code utilizes a recursive approach for this:
- If `b` is 0, `a` is the GCD.
- Otherwise, the GCD is found by recursively calling `gcd` with `b` and the remainder of `a` divided by `b` (`a % b`).
