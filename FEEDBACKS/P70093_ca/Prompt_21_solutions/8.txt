### Feedback for the "Fusió de taules" Program

This feedback addresses the provided Python program designed to merge two sorted lists.

#### 1. Summary of the Problem and Solution Approach

The problem "Fusió de taules" requires implementing a function, `fusio`, that takes two already sorted lists of floating-point numbers (`v1` and `v2`) as input. The goal is to merge these two lists into a single new list, which must also be sorted in ascending order, and importantly, must retain all duplicate elements from both original lists.

The implemented solution employs a classic **two-pointer merge algorithm**. This approach initializes two pointers (or indices), `i1` and `i2`, pointing to the beginning of `v1` and `v2` respectively. It iteratively compares the elements at these pointers. The smaller of the two elements is appended to a new `llista_definitiva` (final list), and its corresponding pointer is advanced. This process continues until one of the lists is fully traversed. Finally, any remaining elements from the other list are appended to the `llista_definitiva`.
