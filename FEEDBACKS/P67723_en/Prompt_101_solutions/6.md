## Problem and Solution Approach

The problem requires finding the largest positive integer that divides two given positive integers without leaving a remainder. The provided solution attempts to solve this using the Euclidean Algorithm, which is a very efficient method for calculating the GCD. The core idea of the Euclidean Algorithm is that the GCD of two numbers does not change if the larger number is replaced by its difference with the smaller number. This process is repeated until the two numbers are equal, which is then the GCD. A more optimized version uses the modulo operator, where the larger number is replaced by its remainder when divided by the smaller number.
